# SteadyStep — PRD

## Problem
Falls in adults 65+ cause ~87,000 hospitalizations/year in Switzerland. Direct costs exceed CHF 1 billion/year. Current solutions are reactive (alert after fall). Fear of falling reduces activity, leading to isolation and loss of independence. No dominant fall-prevention app exists in Swiss market.

## Solution
Smartphone exercise coach with daily Otago-derived balance/strength sessions (10-15 min) led by a conversational AI companion. On-device inference for privacy. Adapts difficulty. Tracks progress over time.

## Target user
- Primary: Adults 65+ living independently in Switzerland
- Secondary: Family members with visibility into parent's activity
- Buyer: Swiss health insurers (reimbursement) or senior direct (self-pay)

## Differentiators
- On-device AI for privacy and low latency
- Swiss-specific: multilingual, FADP-compliant, KVG reimbursement-ready
- Voice-based passive progress tracking as biomarker
- Clinical evidence pathway built into GTM

## Prototype scope
This is a DEMO, not an MVP. It exists to show a clinical co-founder how the product could feel. It needs to:
1. Feel like a real app on a phone
2. Show daily exercise flow clearly
3. Demonstrate AI companion (warm, adaptive)
4. Display credible progress tracking
5. Hint at balance-check feature without real sensors

Does NOT need: backend, real AI inference, real sensors, auth, multilanguage.

## Success criteria
Surgeon taps through for 5 minutes and says "I can see my patients using this." He identifies at least one geriatrician or physio to connect.

## Out of scope
Real AI, real IMU, auth, cloud sync, multilanguage, family portal, clinical advice.

## Voice
Encouraging, calm, respectful. Never patronizing. Tagline: "Your daily balance companion."
